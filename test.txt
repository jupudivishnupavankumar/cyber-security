Lab Tutorial: Cyberattack Case Study and Mitigation Strategies
Objective: Analyze a recent cyberattack, identify the cause, and propose prevention methods.

Lab Summary:
In this lab session, you will delve into a case study of a recent cyberattack. You will select a cyberattack of your choice from the provided list and identify its origins, exploited weaknesses, and prevention measures.
You must officially record your information within this document instead of solely obtaining data from a web server. Make sure this document is in your weekly code folder.
Lab Instructions:

Step 1: Choose Your Attack
Each student will pick one cyberattack from the following list:
SolarWinds Supply Chain Attack (2020) - Attackers embed a backdoor in SolarWinds' Orion software, affecting thousands of organizations.
Colonial Pipeline Ransomware Attack (2021) - A ransomware attack disrupts fuel supplies along the Eastern United States coastline.
Log4j Vulnerability Exploit (2021) - A critical flaw in Log4j enables remote code execution across numerous systems.
Twitter Bitcoin Scam (2020) - Hackers compromise high-profile Twitter accounts and promote a Bitcoin scam.
Uber Data Breach (2022) - Social engineering is used to gain unauthorized access to Uber's internal systems.
T-Mobile Data Breach (2023) - API vulnerabilities are exploited by attackers to steal customer data.
CrowdStrike IT Outages (2024) - In March 2024, after a stress test, CrowdStrike releases Rapid Response Content for Channel File 291. Updates in April 2024 result in IT outages for some users, prompting investigations and explanations from the company.
Microsoft Exchange Server Hack (2021) - State-sponsored attackers exploit Microsoft Exchange server vulnerabilities.
MOVEit File Transfer Hack (2023) - A zero-day vulnerability in the MOVEit file transfer software is exploited to steal data.
WannaCry Ransomware Attack (2017) - A global ransomware attack exploits a Microsoft Windows vulnerability, affecting thousands of organizations worldwide, including the UK's NHS.
Zoom Security Issues (2020) - Amid the rise of remote work, cybercriminals exploit vulnerabilities in Zoom's video conferencing software, leading to "Zoombombing" incidents and privacy concerns related to encryption.
23andMe data breach (2022) - Unauthorized access is gained to the personal data of some genetic testing company users.
Kaseya VSA Ransomware Attack (2021) - Attackers target the Kaseya VSA platform, used by managed service providers, and infect their clients with ransomware, affecting hundreds of businesses and organizations globally.

Step 2: Research the Attack
Describe how the attack occurred and identify the specific vulnerabilities or weaknesses exploited.
If known, identify the attackers, including whether they were a nation-state, criminal organization, or individual hackers.
Discuss the financial, operational, and reputational impact of the attack.
Explain how the attack was discovered by organizations or security researchers.

Step 3: Determine the Cause and Mitigation
Explain why the attack was successful and identify any security gaps, misconfigurations, or lack of updates.
Suggest methods and solutions to prevent similar attacks in the future.
Outline the security controls that should be implemented to prevent future attacks, such as network monitoring, incident response plans, and user awareness training.

Step 4: Collaborate with a Peer
Share your findings with at least one other student and discuss your results.

Lab Logbook Requirements:
Record the following information in your lab logbook:
The type of attack you have chosen.
A link to one key website or research paper that you found useful.

Optional Activity: You may analyze more than one type of attack.
